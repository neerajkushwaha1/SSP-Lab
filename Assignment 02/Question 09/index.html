About Rome

Rome, Italy's capital, is a sprawling, cosmopolitan city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture and culture on display. Ancient ruins such as the Forum and the Colosseum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire.

Vatican City, headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church, has St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums, which house masterpieces such as Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel frescoes.

The city is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, delicious cuisine, and vibrant culture. From ancient ruins to Renaissance masterpieces, Rome offers a journey through time unlike any other city in the world.
